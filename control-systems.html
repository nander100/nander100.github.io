Project Overview

As a Systems and Control Engineer at UCR's Robotics and Medical Systems Lab, I specialized in the development of precision control systems for medical robotics applications, focusing on MRI-compatible steerable needles and biopsy robots. Since the robot needed to be MRI MRI-compatible, there could be no metals or electronics in the opperating room. We decided to use fiber optics and pnumatics to control the robot. The air would be compressed by a stepper motor which would correspond to a certian ammout of bend for the needle. I was tasked with calibrating the sensor and developing a control system to carry out this task.

Technical Achievements

  • PID Integration:

    Worked with a PhD student (Boshen Qi) and a postdoc researcher (Sarvesh Saini) to develop a PID controler to control the bend of a steerable robot.

  • Fiber Optic Sensor Calibration:

    Calibrated and fine-tuned MRI-compatible fiber optic proximity sensors using SIMULINK to measure needle deflection with precision better than 0.5mm using a stepper motor. This level of accuracy is critical for medical procedures requiring high precision in tissue targeting.

Research Contributions

My work contributed directly to the publication "An MRI-conditional flexible endoscopic robot with a hydraulic tendon-driven actuation system" in the Journal of Medical Robotics Research. The control systems I developed as well as my sensor calibration formed a critical component of the robot's functionality, enabling precise manipulation in the MRI environment.

The overall, I found that the sensor was most consistent at 20.0mm to 45.0mm. This was used as a crucial component for the feedback mechanism of the PID controller.
