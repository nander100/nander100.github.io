Organization Overview

As the Co-Founder and President of Highlander Combat Robotics at UC Riverside, I've led the development of a thriving robotics community focused on designing, building, and competing with combat robots. Our organization provides engineering students with hands-on experience in mechanical design, electrical systems, and programming while fostering teamwork and project management skills.

Key Achievements

  • Organization Founding:

    Established Highlander Combat Robotics from the ground up, developing the club constitution, securing university recognition, and building a membership base of over 40 active participants from diverse engineering disciplines.

  • Funding and Sponsorships:

    Secured over $5,000 in sponsorships and funding through grant writing, industry partnerships, and university resources. These funds enabled the purchase of equipment, materials, and the construction of a competition arena.

  • Technical Workshops:

    Developed and led workshops on SolidWorks CAD modeling, Arduino programming, motor control systems, and safety protocols. These educational initiatives increased member retention by 50% and ensured all participants gained valuable technical skills regardless of prior experience.

  • Annual Competition:

    Organized and executed UCR's first combat robotics competition, featuring six student-created robots and attracting over 40 spectators. The event included multiple weight classes and provided students with real-world engineering competition experience.

  • Safety Innovations:

    Engineered and implemented critical safety systems including an emergency power-off system for combat robots, custom wiring harnesses with built-in protection circuits, and comprehensive safety training for all team members.
